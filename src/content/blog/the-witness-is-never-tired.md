There is a quiet experiment available to anyone, at any hour. Notice that the eyes grow tired, but the seeing does not. Notice that thoughts arrive heavy and leave light, but the one who notices them is unchanged by either weight. This is not a metaphor. It is a description of something you can check, right now, without moving.

The Ashtavakra Gita speaks plainly about this. It does not argue. It points. When Janaka asks how liberation is possible, the answer is not a technique. The answer is an observation — that he is, and has always been, what he is looking for.

> You are not the body, you are not the mind. You are awareness itself, vast and free.

## What changes and what does not

Every state changes. Waking gives way to dreaming, dreaming to sleep, sleep back to waking. Moods rise and fall. The body ages, sometimes hour to hour. And yet something registers all of this without itself being any of it. That registering is not a thought. It is the field in which thoughts appear.

The mistake — and it is a strangely consistent one across lifetimes — is to identify with the content rather than the field. To say "I am tired" when what is meant is "tiredness is being noticed." The grammar of language hides this. The grammar of awareness does not.

---

None of this requires belief. It requires only a willingness to look once, carefully, and then again, more carefully, at what is actually happening when something is happening.
